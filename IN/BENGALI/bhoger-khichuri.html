Ingredients

  • 1 cup gobindobhog rice (or any small grain aromatic rice)
  • 1/2 cup yellow moong dal (dry roasted)
  • 1 medium potato (cubed)
  • 1/2 cup cauliflower florets
  • 1/2 cup green peas
  • 1 tomato (chopped)
  • 1-inch ginger (grated or paste)
  • 2â€“3 green chilies
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 dried red chilies
  • 4 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
  • 2 tbsp mustard oil (optional)
  • Salt and sugar to taste

Instructions

  1. Wash and soak the rice and moong dal separately for 15 minutes. Drain before cooking.
  2. Dry roast moong dal until it emits a nutty aroma. Set aside.
  3. Heat ghee (and mustard oil if using) in a deep pot. Add bay leaves, cumin seeds, dried red chilies.
  4. Add grated ginger and chopped tomato. Cook until soft.
  5. Add turmeric, salt, sugar, and the vegetables. SautÃ© for a few minutes.
  6. Add roasted dal and rice. Mix well.
  7. Pour 4 cups of hot water and bring it to a boil. Add green chilies.
  8. Cover and cook on low heat until rice and dal are soft and slightly mushy.
  9. Finish with a spoonful of ghee on top. Serve hot.

Serve With

  • Labra (mixed vegetable curry)
  • Beguni (batter-fried eggplant slices)
  • Chutney and papad
