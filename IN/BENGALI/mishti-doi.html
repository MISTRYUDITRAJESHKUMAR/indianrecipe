Ingredients

  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 200g sugar
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt (for the culture)
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder (optional)
  • 1 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)

Instructions

  1. Heat the milk in a heavy-bottomed pan over medium heat. Once it comes to a boil, reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes to thicken slightly.
  2. In another small pan, heat the ghee and add sugar. Stir continuously until the sugar melts completely and turns into a light caramel (golden color).
  3. Add the melted sugar syrup to the simmering milk and stir well. Allow the milk to cool down to room temperature.
  4. Once the milk is lukewarm, add the yogurt and cardamom powder (optional), and mix gently to incorporate the culture.
  5. Transfer the milk mixture into a clay pot or serving bowl.
  6. Cover with a lid and place it in a warm, undisturbed spot for 6-8 hours or overnight to allow it to set. The yogurt should firm up and turn sweet.
  7. Once set, refrigerate for a couple of hours before serving for a chilled treat.

Serve With

  • As a dessert or snack.
üí° Tip: Mishti Doi tastes best when made in a clay pot, which enhances its flavor and texture.
