Ingredients

  • 4 cups raw mango (cut into small cubes)
  • 1/2 cup salt (non-iodized)
  • 1/2 cup mustard seeds (split or crushed)
  • 2 tbsp fenugreek seeds (methi dana)
  • 1/2 cup red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/4 tsp asafoetida (hing)
  • 1.5 to 2 cups mustard oil

Instructions

  1. Wash and dry the mango pieces thoroughly. Let them air-dry to remove moisture.
  2. Mix mango cubes with salt and turmeric. Keep in a jar for 2 days, stirring once daily.
  3. After 2 days, add crushed mustard, fenugreek seeds, red chili powder, and hing. Mix well.
  4. Heat mustard oil until smoking. Let it cool completely before pouring over the mango mixture.
  5. Mix well and store in a clean, dry glass jar. Keep in a warm place for 7â€“10 days for proper fermentation.
  6. Once ready, refrigerate or store in a cool dry place. Stir occasionally with a clean spoon.

Serve With

  • Thepla, puri, or paratha
  • Gujarati thali meals
  • Khichdi or plain rice
