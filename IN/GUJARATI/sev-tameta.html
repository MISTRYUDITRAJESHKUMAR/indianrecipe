Ingredients

  • 4 ripe tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup thick sev (fried chickpea flour noodles)
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/4 tsp asafoetida (hing)
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander-cumin powder
  • 1 tsp sugar (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander for garnish
  • 1/2 cup water

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add cumin seeds and a pinch of asafoetida.
  3. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and coriander-cumin powder. Stir for a few seconds.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft and mushy.
  5. Add salt, sugar (if using), and water. Bring to a boil.
  6. Just before serving, add sev and mix lightly.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve hot.

Serve With

  • Rotli (roti)
  • Paratha
  • Plain rice
üí° Tip: Add sev only at the end to retain its crunch.
