Ingredients

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 1/4 cup oil or ghee
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 2-3 cardamom pods
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 1/4 cup yogurt
  • 1/4 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1/4 cup mint leaves, chopped

Instructions

  1. Wash and soak the basmati rice for 30 minutes, then drain.
  2. Heat oil or ghee in a large pan. Add cumin seeds, bay leaf, cloves, cardamom, and cinnamon stick. Saut√© until aromatic.
  3. Add the sliced onions and saut√© until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, and saut√© for another minute.
  4. Stir in turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Cook for a minute to release the spices' aroma.
  5. Add the soaked rice and saut√© for 2-3 minutes to coat the rice with the spices.
  6. Pour in 1 1/2 cups water and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat, cover the pan, and let it cook for 15-18 minutes, or until the rice is tender and water is absorbed.
  7. Once cooked, turn off the heat and let the rice sit for 5 minutes. Then, gently fluff the rice with a fork.
  8. Stir in yogurt, fresh coriander, and mint leaves for added flavor and fragrance.
  9. Serve hot as a side dish or main course with your favorite curry or raita.

Serve With

  • Hyderabadi Biryani
  • Raita
  • Salad
