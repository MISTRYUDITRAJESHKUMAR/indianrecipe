üçû Double Ka Meetha

Ingredients

  • 6 slices of bread (preferably white or milk bread)
  • 1/2 cup ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/4 cup khoya (optional, for richness)
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1/4 tsp saffron strands (optional)
  • 1/4 cup cashews, chopped
  • 1/4 cup almonds, slivered
  • 1/4 cup pistachios, chopped (optional)
  • 1 tbsp rose water or kewra water (optional)

Instructions

  1. Trim the edges of the bread slices and cut them into halves or quarters as preferred.
  2. Heat ghee in a pan and shallow fry the bread slices until they turn golden brown and crispy. Remove and set aside on paper towels to drain excess ghee.
  3. In a separate saucepan, combine sugar and water to make the sugar syrup. Heat on low flame and stir until the sugar dissolves. Add cardamom powder and saffron (if using). Let it simmer for a few minutes, then remove from heat.
  4. In another pan, heat the milk and add khoya (if using) to make a rich milk mixture. Stir until the khoya melts and combines with the milk. Add a few drops of rose water or kewra water for fragrance, if desired.
  5. To assemble, arrange the fried bread slices in a serving dish. Pour the sugar syrup evenly over the bread slices, allowing them to soak well.
  6. Next, pour the warm milk and khoya mixture over the soaked bread slices.
  7. Garnish with chopped cashews, almonds, and pistachios.
  8. Let the Double Ka Meetha sit for a while to allow the flavors to blend, then serve warm or chilled.

Serve With

  • Ice cream
  • Sweet lassi
  • Fruit salad
üí° Tip: For a more decadent version, you can add a layer of whipped cream or top with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.
