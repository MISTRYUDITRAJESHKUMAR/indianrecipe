Ingredients

  • 500g boneless mutton (or chicken)
  • 1 cup broken wheat (soaked for 4 hours)
  • 1/2 cup chana dal
  • 1/2 cup masoor dal
  • 1/2 cup moong dal
  • 2 onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander and mint leaves
  • Ghee or oil as needed
  • Fried onions for garnish
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Instructions

  1. Cook soaked wheat and dals with water and a pinch of turmeric until soft and mushy. Blend slightly for a coarse texture.
  2. Heat ghee or oil, fry onions until golden. Add ginger-garlic paste, meat, chili powder, turmeric, and salt. Cook until meat browns.
  3. Add yogurt and cook until oil separates. Add the blended wheat-dal mixture and combine well.
  4. Simmer on low heat for 30‚Äì40 minutes, stirring frequently to prevent sticking.
  5. Add garam masala, coriander, and mint. Mix and simmer for another 10 minutes.
  6. Serve hot garnished with fried onions, lemon wedges, and more herbs.

Serve With

  • Naan or paratha
  • Lemon wedges
  • Onion rings
üí° Tip: Slow cooking and continuous stirring gives haleem its unique creamy texture.
