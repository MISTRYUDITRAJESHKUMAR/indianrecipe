Ingredients

  • 1 kg mutton (bone-in, cleaned)
  • 2 cups basmati rice (soaked for 30 minutes)
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 cup fried onions
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Whole spices (bay leaf, cinnamon, cloves, cardamom)
  • Few saffron strands soaked in warm milk
  • Fresh coriander and mint leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 tbsp ghee or oil

Instructions

  1. Marinate mutton with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, chili powder, garam masala, salt, and fried onions. Refrigerate overnight.
  2. Boil water with salt and whole spices. Parboil the rice (70% cooked) and drain.
  3. In a heavy-bottom pan, layer marinated mutton, herbs, and partially cooked rice.
  4. Drizzle saffron milk and ghee on top. Cover tightly with a lid and seal with dough.
  5. Cook on high flame for 10 minutes, then on low heat for 45 minutes (Dum cooking).
  6. Let it rest for 10 minutes before serving. Mix gently and serve hot.

Serve With

  • Onion Raita
  • Mirchi ka Salan
  • Salad
