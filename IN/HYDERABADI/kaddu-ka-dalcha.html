Ingredients

  • 250g pumpkin (kaddu), peeled and cubed
  • 1/2 cup toor dal (yellow pigeon peas)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 green chili, slit
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)
  • 1 tbsp tamarind pulp (optional, for tanginess)

Instructions

  1. Wash the toor dal thoroughly and cook it in a pressure cooker with 2 cups of water for 3-4 whistles or until soft. Set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and curry leaves. Once they splutter, add chopped onions and sautÃ© until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and sautÃ© for another 2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes, green chili, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Cook until the tomatoes become soft and the oil begins to separate from the masala.
  5. Add the cubed pumpkin pieces and stir well. Cook for 5-7 minutes, allowing the pumpkin to soften.
  6. Once the pumpkin is cooked, add the cooked toor dal to the pan. Mix well and add water as needed to achieve the desired consistency of the dalcha.
  7. Let the dalcha simmer on low heat for 10 minutes. Adjust salt and spice levels to taste.
  8. If using tamarind pulp, add it to the dalcha and mix well. Cook for another 5 minutes to let the flavors blend.
  9. Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
  10. Serve hot with steamed rice or roti.

Serve With

  • Steamed rice
  • Roti or paratha
  • Salad and raita
