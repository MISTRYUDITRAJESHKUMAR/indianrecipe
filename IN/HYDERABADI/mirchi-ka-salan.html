Ingredients

  • 8‚Äì10 long green chilies (preferably medium-hot)
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tbsp tamarind paste or 1/2 cup tamarind pulp
  • 1/2 cup peanuts
  • 2 tbsp sesame seeds
  • 2 tbsp grated coconut (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 cup water (adjust to desired consistency)
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a pan and fry the green chilies until slightly blistered. Remove and set aside.
  2. In the same pan, add the remaining oil and saut√© chopped onions until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 2 minutes, then add all powdered spices (turmeric, red chili, coriander, cumin) and fry for another minute.
  4. Grind the peanuts, sesame seeds, and coconut into a smooth paste, adding a little water if needed.
  5. Add the ground paste to the pan and cook for 3-4 minutes until the oil starts to separate from the masala.
  6. Add tamarind paste, water, and salt. Stir well and bring to a simmer for 5 minutes.
  7. Carefully add the fried chilies into the gravy and cook on low heat for another 5-7 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend.
  8. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve hot.

Serve With

  • Hyderabadi Biryani
  • Steamed basmati rice
  • Chapati or naan
