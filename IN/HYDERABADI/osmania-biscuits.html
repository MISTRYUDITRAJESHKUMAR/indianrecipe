Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 cup semolina (sooji)
  • 1/4 cup butter, softened
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/4 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/4 cup milk (or as needed)
  • 1 tsp rose water (optional, for fragrance)
  • 1 tbsp milk (for brushing the biscuits)
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds (optional, for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 180¬∞C (350¬∞F) and line a baking tray with parchment paper.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, semolina, baking powder, baking soda, cardamom powder, and salt. Mix well.
  3. Add the softened butter and powdered sugar to the dry ingredients. Rub the butter into the flour mixture using your fingertips until it resembles breadcrumbs.
  4. Gradually add milk, a tablespoon at a time, to form a soft dough. The dough should be smooth and pliable but not too sticky.
  5. Roll the dough out onto a lightly floured surface to about 1/4 inch thickness. Using a cookie cutter or the rim of a glass, cut out round shapes for the biscuits.
  6. Place the biscuits on the prepared baking tray. Brush each biscuit with a little milk and sprinkle with sesame seeds if desired.
  7. Bake the biscuits in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes or until they turn golden brown at the edges.
  8. Allow the biscuits to cool on a wire rack before serving.

Serve With

  • Irani Chai
  • Milk tea
  • Cold coffee
