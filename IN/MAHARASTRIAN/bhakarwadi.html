Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 cup rice flour
  • 2 tbsp semolina (rava)
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp carom seeds (ajwain)
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/4 cup warm oil (for dough)
  • Water (as needed to make dough)
  • 1/2 cup fresh grated coconut
  • 1 tbsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tbsp tamarind paste
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions

  1. Mix all-purpose flour, rice flour, semolina, turmeric, cumin seeds, carom seeds, red chili powder, sesame seeds, and salt in a bowl.
  2. Gradually add warm oil and mix it into the flour until it resembles breadcrumbs.
  3. Slowly add water and knead the dough into a smooth and firm consistency. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  4. For the filling, mix the grated coconut, coriander powder, cumin powder, garam masala, tamarind paste, and sugar in a separate bowl.
  5. Divide the dough into small balls. Roll each ball into a thin sheet and spread the filling evenly over the surface.
  6. Carefully roll up the sheet into a cylindrical shape and seal the edges.
  7. Cut the rolled dough into small, bite-sized pieces (about 1-1.5 inches long).
  8. Heat oil in a pan and deep fry the bhakarwadi pieces until golden brown and crispy.
  9. Remove from oil and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  10. Allow the bhakarwadi to cool completely before storing them in an airtight container.

Serve With

  • Chai (tea)
  • Mint chutney (optional)
