Ingredients

  • 6‚Äì8 small brinjals (eggplants)
  • 1/2 cup grated dry coconut
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds
  • 1 tbsp peanuts
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp goda masala or garam masala
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp asafoetida (hing)
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

Instructions

  1. Dry roast peanuts, coconut, and sesame seeds. Let cool and grind into a coarse powder.
  2. Mix in chopped onions, goda masala, chili powder, turmeric, and salt to form the stuffing.
  3. Make a criss-cross slit in each brinjal, keeping the stem intact. Stuff the masala into each brinjal.
  4. Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them crackle, then add hing.
  5. Gently place the stuffed brinjals in the pan. Add any leftover masala.
  6. Cover and cook on low flame for 15‚Äì20 minutes or until the brinjals are soft and cooked.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve hot.

Serve With

  • Jowar or Bajra Bhakri
  • Steamed rice and dal
  • Pickle and curd
üí° Tip: For authentic flavor, use Maharashtrian goda masala and cook in a heavy-bottomed pan on low flame.
