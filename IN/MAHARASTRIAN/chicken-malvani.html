Ingredients

  • 500g chicken pieces (preferably with bone)
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 dried red chilies
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tbsp Malvani masala (or substitute with garam masala)
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk (or grated coconut)
  • 1 cup water (adjust for gravy consistency)
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • 1 tbsp lime juice

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pot and add cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add dried red chilies and chopped onions. Saut√© until the onions turn golden brown.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for another minute. Then, add chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and the oil separates.
  3. Now, add the Malvani masala (or garam masala), coriander powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Stir well to combine.
  4. Add the chicken pieces and cook on medium heat until the chicken turns slightly brown.
  5. Pour in the coconut milk (or grated coconut) and 1 cup of water. Mix well and cover. Let it cook for 25-30 minutes or until the chicken is tender and cooked through.
  6. If you prefer a thicker gravy, you can cook it uncovered for a few more minutes to reduce the sauce.
  7. Once the chicken is cooked, add lime juice and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

Serve With

  • Chapati or Bhakri
  • Steamed Rice
  • Onion Salad
üí° Tip: Adjust the amount of Malvani masala to control the spice level of the dish. You can also add a dash of tamarind paste for added tanginess.
