Ingredients

  • 6 boiled eggs, peeled
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1-2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1/2 cup water (adjust for desired consistency)
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • 1 tbsp lime juice

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pan, add cumin seeds, and let them splutter. Then, add chopped onions and sautÃ© until golden brown.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for another minute. Then, add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and the oil starts separating from the masala.
  3. Now, add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Stir well and cook for another 2 minutes.
  4. Add 1/2 cup of water and bring the mixture to a simmer. Let the gravy cook for 5-10 minutes until it thickens slightly.
  5. Gently add the boiled eggs to the gravy. Cut a few slits on the eggs to let the gravy seep in. Cover and cook for 5 minutes.
  6. Once the eggs are well coated with the curry, add garam masala and lime juice. Stir gently and cook for another 2 minutes.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot.

Serve With

  • Chapati or Naan
  • Steamed Rice
  • Onion Salad
