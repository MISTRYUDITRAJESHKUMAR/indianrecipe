Ingredients

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1/2 cup jaggery (grated)
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1/4 tsp nutmeg powder (optional)
  • 1 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
  • Water (for kneading dough)
  • Ghee for cooking

Instructions

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the whole wheat flour, cardamom powder, and nutmeg powder (if using).
  2. Add grated jaggery and mix well. Add water little by little and knead the dough until soft and smooth.
  3. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
  4. Divide the dough into small balls (about the size of a lime).
  5. Roll each dough ball into a small, round disc (like a flatbread).
  6. Heat a tawa or griddle on medium heat. Once hot, place the rolled-out dough onto the tawa and cook for a minute or until small bubbles start to appear on the surface.
  7. Flip the poli and apply ghee on the cooked side. Cook the other side for 30 seconds, and flip again to cook the first side until golden brown.
  8. Once both sides are cooked, remove the poli from the tawa. Repeat for the remaining dough balls.
  9. Serve hot, ideally with a dollop of ghee or as a sweet treat.

Serve With

  • Ghee
  • Sweet curd
