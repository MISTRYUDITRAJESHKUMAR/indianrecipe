Ingredients

  • 1 cup rice flour
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 cup grated coconut (fresh or desiccated)
  • 1/2 cup jaggery (or sugar)
  • 1 tbsp poppy seeds (optional)
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp ghee for greasing

Instructions

  1. In a pan, heat 1/2 cup water, add a pinch of salt, and bring it to a boil.
  2. Add rice flour to the boiling water, stirring continuously to avoid lumps. Keep stirring until it forms a dough-like consistency.
  3. Once the dough comes together, add 1 tbsp ghee and cardamom powder. Mix well and let the dough cool slightly.
  4. Meanwhile, in another pan, heat 1 tbsp ghee, and add grated coconut, jaggery, poppy seeds, and sesame seeds. Cook until the jaggery melts and the mixture thickens. Set this mixture aside to cool.
  5. Once the dough is cool enough to handle, grease your palms with ghee and divide the dough into small portions.
  6. Flatten each portion into a small circle. Place a spoonful of the coconut-jaggery filling in the center of each circle.
  7. Carefully fold the edges of the dough over the filling, shaping them into small dumplings with a peak at the top (like a conical shape).
  8. Steam the modaks in a steamer or idli maker for 10-15 minutes, until they are soft and cooked through.
  9. Serve hot with a drizzle of ghee or enjoy them as is!

Serve With

  • Fresh Coconut Milk
  • Sweetened Milk
