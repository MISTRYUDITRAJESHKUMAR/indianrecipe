Ingredients

  • 500g mutton (or chicken) pieces with bone
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tbsp freshly grated coconut
  • 1-2 green chilies, chopped (optional)
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 cup water (adjust for desired gravy consistency)
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • 1 tbsp lime juice

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pot and add cumin seeds. Let them splutter, then add chopped onions and saut√© until they turn golden brown.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and chopped green chilies (if using) and cook for another minute.
  3. Now, add coriander powder, turmeric powder, black pepper powder, and salt. Stir well and cook until the oil starts separating from the masala.
  4. Grind the grated coconut into a smooth paste and add it to the pot. Mix well with the spices.
  5. Add the mutton (or chicken) pieces to the pot and sear them on all sides.
  6. Add about 1 cup of water, mix well, and cover. Cook on medium heat for about 40-45 minutes or until the meat is tender and cooked through. Add more water as needed to adjust the consistency of the gravy.
  7. Once the meat is cooked, add lime juice and stir to combine.
  8. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot.

Serve With

  • Chapati or Bhakri
  • Steamed Rice
  • Onion Salad
üí° Tip: Pandhra Rassa has a milder flavor compared to its counterpart Tambda Rassa. You can adjust the spice levels by adding more or fewer green chilies.
