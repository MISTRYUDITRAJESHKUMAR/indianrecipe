Ingredients

  • 1 cup chana dal (split Bengal gram)
  • 1/2 cup jaggery or sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1/2 tsp nutmeg powder (optional)
  • 1 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder (optional)
  • Water as needed
  • Ghee for frying

Instructions

  1. Wash and soak the chana dal in water for 30 minutes to 1 hour.
  2. Drain the water and pressure cook the chana dal with 2 cups of water until soft but not mushy (around 3-4 whistles).
  3. Drain any excess water from the cooked chana dal and transfer it to a pan. Add jaggery or sugar and cook on medium heat until the jaggery dissolves and the mixture thickens.
  4. Add cardamom powder, nutmeg powder, and 1 tbsp ghee. Stir well and cook for another 5-10 minutes until the mixture becomes thick and dry. Let the puran (filling) cool.
  5. For the dough, mix the whole wheat flour, turmeric powder, and a pinch of salt in a bowl. Gradually add water and knead into a soft dough. Let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
  6. Divide the dough and the puran filling into small equal-sized portions.
  7. Take a portion of dough, flatten it into a small disc, and place a portion of puran in the center. Seal the edges and roll it out gently into a flat round disc.
  8. Heat a tawa or griddle and cook the puran poli on both sides, applying ghee, until golden brown spots appear on both sides.
  9. Serve hot with more ghee on top or with a side of yogurt or milk.

Serve With

  • Sweetened yogurt
  • Milk
  • Ghee
