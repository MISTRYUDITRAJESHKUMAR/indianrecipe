Ingredients

  • 500g mutton (or chicken) pieces with bone
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tbsp red chili powder (adjust to spice preference)
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 cup water (adjust for desired gravy consistency)
  • 1 tbsp tamarind paste or lemon juice
  • 1/4 tsp garam masala
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pot and add chopped onions. Saut√© until the onions turn golden brown.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for another minute, then add the chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
  3. Now, add red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, and salt. Stir well and cook until the oil separates from the masala.
  4. Put in the mutton pieces (or chicken) and sear the meat on all sides.
  5. Add 1 cup of water, mix well, and cover the pot. Cook on medium heat for about 30-40 minutes, or until the meat is tender and cooked through. Add more water if needed to adjust the consistency of the gravy.
  6. Once the meat is tender, add tamarind paste or lemon juice and garam masala. Stir to combine and cook for an additional 5 minutes to let the flavors blend.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot.

Serve With

  • Chapati or Bhakri
  • Steamed Rice
  • Onion Salad
üí° Tip: For extra depth of flavor, let the rassa rest for a few hours before serving.
