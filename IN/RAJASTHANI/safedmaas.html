Ingredients

  • 500g mutton (bone-in)
  • 3 tbsp ghee or oil
  • 2 medium onions (sliced)
  • 1 cup curd (whisked)
  • 1/4 cup cashew paste
  • 1/4 cup poppy seed paste (optional)
  • 1 tsp ginger paste
  • 1 tsp garlic paste
  • 1 bay leaf, 2-3 cloves, 2 green cardamoms
  • 1 small cinnamon stick
  • 1/2 tsp white pepper powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cream (optional) for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat ghee/oil in a pan and add whole spices. Saut√© until aromatic.
  2. Add onions and cook until soft and translucent (not browned).
  3. Stir in ginger-garlic paste. Add mutton and sear on high flame.
  4. Lower the flame and add cashew and poppy seed paste. Mix well.
  5. Add curd gradually while stirring to avoid curdling.
  6. Season with salt and white pepper. Cover and cook on low heat until mutton is tender.
  7. Add a splash of water if needed. Finish with fresh cream if desired.

Serve With

  • Steamed rice or butter naan
  • Mint chutney and sliced onions
ü§ç Tip: For an authentic Rajasthani flavor, use poppy seeds and cook on low flame for richness.
